Composite Index

The Food Vulnerability Index combines three urban conditions: economic pressure, food consumption intensity, and household food storage capacity. These layers were normalized and synthesized to identify neighborhoods where food stress is structurally concentrated. The index does not measure access alone, but the ability of neighborhoods to absorb disruption.

Key Findings

Vulnerability Clusters

  • Food vulnerability clusters in areas with high consumption, high poverty, and low storage.
  • These neighborhoods depend heavily on continuous food flows and delivery systems.
  • When disruption occurs, they are structurally more exposed to supply, price, and climate shocks.

Risk

Mapping is not the endpoint of this project. The goal is not only to show where vulnerability exists, but to identify where everyday decisions can intervene. Food risk is lived through daily behavior—shopping frequency, substitution, storage, and waste. These decision points become the operational entry points for design.
