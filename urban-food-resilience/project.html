Project Overview

Understanding Urban Food Vulnerability and Resilience

Research Context

Urban food systems face increasing pressure from climate change, supply chain disruption, and spatial constraints. This project examines how cities, particularly New York, can develop more resilient and low-carbon food networks.

Key Questions

  • How does food vulnerability manifest in urban environments?
  • What role can urban design play in strengthening food resilience?
  • How can low-carbon food systems be integrated into existing urban infrastructure?

Research Scope

This atlas focuses on New York City, analyzing existing food systems, identifying vulnerable populations, and proposing design interventions for sustainable urban agriculture and food distribution.
