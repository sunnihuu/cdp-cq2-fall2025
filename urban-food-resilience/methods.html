Research Methods

Combining spatial analysis, data collection, and design research

Spatial Analysis

Geographic Information Systems (GIS) mapping techniques were employed to visualize food distribution networks, urban agriculture sites, and areas of food vulnerability across New York City.

[ Map visualization would be embedded here ]

Data Collection

  • Public health and nutrition datasets
  • Urban land use and zoning data
  • Transportation and infrastructure networks
  • Socioeconomic indicators by neighborhood
  • Case studies of existing food initiatives

Design Research

Qualitative analysis combined with visual synthesis to develop design interventions that address identified vulnerabilities. Architectural prototypes and urban concepts were tested against sustainability and feasibility criteria.

Stakeholder Engagement

Research informed by conversations with urban planners, food policy advocates, and community organizations working on food justice and resilience in New York City.
